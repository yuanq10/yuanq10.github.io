My name is Qihang Yuan, and I also go by Logan. I am a Bachelor of Software and Biomedical Engineering graduate from McMaster University, Class of 2025. I have a strong interest in technology, with a current focus on artificial intelligence and interactive mobile experiences. I believe AI will play a transformative role in how we live and work over the next decade, and that the most critical skill lies not only in understanding how these tools function, but in applying them responsibly and effectively. This perspective strongly shapes the direction of my learning and career development.

I'm particularly interested in the role of AI in automating daily activities. As AI technologies continue to advance, I believe an increasing number of routine tasks will be augmented or fully automated, making the ability to understand and effectively leverage these tools essential for the future workforce. Developing both technical literacy and thoughtful application of AI is, in my view, key to adapting to and shaping this transition.

I'm also deeply interested in human–machine interfacing. With continued progress in algorithms and a deeper understanding of human neuroscience, I believe technologies such as fully controllable external limbs—or even full-body augmentation, as imagined in films like The Terminator—may one day become feasible. Such advancements have the potential to significantly transform healthcare, rehabilitation, and medical treatment, improving quality of life for many.
